Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 25, 2025

Welcome to Spacebar Clicker. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website.

Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access the site.

Quick Summary

Spacebar Clicker is designed with privacy in mind. We do not collect personal information, do not require registration, and do not store your clicking test results. Your usage of our tool is completely anonymous.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Data

We do not collect personal identification information. You can use our Spacebar Clicker tool without providing any personal details such as name, email address, or contact information.

1.2 Non-Personal Data

We may collect non-personal information about your interaction with our website through analytics tools:

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Referring website addresses
  • General geographic location (country/city level)
  • Device type and screen resolution

This information is collected anonymously and cannot be used to identify you personally.

1.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our site. These tools may use cookies, which are small text files stored on your device. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

2. How We Use Your Information

The non-personal information we collect is used to:

  • Improve our website functionality and user experience
  • Analyze usage patterns and trends
  • Monitor and analyze the effectiveness of our service
  • Detect and prevent technical issues
  • Understand which features are most popular

We do NOT use this information to track individual users or create personal profiles.

3. Test Results and Data Storage

Important: Your clicking test results (click counts, timer settings, CPS scores) are NOT stored on our servers. All test data remains local to your browser session and is cleared when you close the page or start a new test. We have no access to your individual test results.

4. Third-Party Services

4.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to collect anonymous usage statistics. Google Analytics may use cookies to track your interaction with our site. For more information about how Google uses data, please visit: Google Privacy Policy

You can opt-out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

4.2 Hosting Services

Our website is hosted on GitHub Pages. Please refer to GitHub's Privacy Statement for information about their data practices.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the information we collect. However, please note that no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. Since we don't collect personal information, the risk to your privacy is minimal.

6. Children's Privacy

Our service is available to users of all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us, and we will take steps to remove such information.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your data:

  • Access: Request information about data we collect (which is minimal and non-personal)
  • Deletion: Request deletion of any data (though we don't store personal data)
  • Opt-out: Disable cookies through your browser settings
  • Do Not Track: Configure your browser to send "Do Not Track" signals

8. International Users

Our website is accessible globally. If you access our site from outside the United States, please be aware that any information collected may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States or other countries where our service providers operate.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal, operational, or regulatory reasons. We will notify users of any material changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

10. Cookie Policy

Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the website to function properly. We use minimal essential cookies only for basic functionality.

Analytics Cookies: These help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information.

Managing Cookies: You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

11. Data Retention

Since we do not collect personal data and test results are not stored on our servers, there is no data retention period for user information. Anonymous analytics data is retained according to Google Analytics' default retention policies.

12. Contact Information

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us:

Contact Page: Visit our Contact Us page

13. Legal Compliance

We comply with applicable data protection laws, including:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for European users
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for California residents
  • Other applicable local privacy regulations
