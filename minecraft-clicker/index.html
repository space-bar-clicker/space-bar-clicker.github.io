This page is an incremental / idle “Minecraft-themed” clicker: you mine blocks, multiply passive “blocks per second,” and reinforce manual mining with upgradeable click power. Mojang/Microsoft’s Minecraft is its own commercial game—this browser build uses familiar pickaxe tiers and vibes as fantasy shorthand for pacing, without claiming lore, multiplayer, caves, villagers, The End, modding parity, or any official tie-in.

What you actually control here

You have two intertwined engines:

  • Manual mining: click the giant pickaxe chunk to add blocks instantly; invest in Click Power so each tap digs deeper digits.
  • Passive mining tiers: stack Stone Pickaxe, Iron Pickaxe, Diamond Pickaxe, Enchantment Table, then Netherite Beacon—each contributes independent block/sec throughput you can buy in multiple stacks with rising per-tier price.

What appears in the UI (accurate list)

  • Stone Pickaxe — +1 block/sec per owned
  • Iron Pickaxe — +5 blocks/sec per owned
  • Diamond Pickaxe — +20 blocks/sec per owned
  • Enchantment Table — +100 blocks/sec per owned
  • Netherite Beacon — +500 blocks/sec per owned
  • Click Power — spend blocks to multiply per-click mining (the “manual DPS button”)
  • Save / Reset — local persistence if you choose to save

That’s why the progression feels like Minecraft’s tech tree in mood but not like a voxel sandbox: you’re optimizing an economy around block income, not surviving creeper ambushes.

How to play (from first click to beacon)

  1. Spam early clicks until you buy the first Stone Pickaxe passives—they pay for every second you aren’t hovering the mouse.
  2. Stack pickaxes before glamor-buying Diamond. Two cheap iron lines can out-DPS waiting for a single beacon if you pause all income.
  3. Enchantment Table is your “factory midgame spike.” +100/sec per unit is the hinge where AFK tabs start to feel worth it.
  4. Netherite Beacon is the apex passive. Once you stack them, manual clicking is mostly for bursts after reset or catching a purchase threshold.
  5. Upgrade click power whenever manual still moves the needle. If passive is still double-digit, doubling clicks wins races to the next tier.
  6. Save often when you’re proud of a run; reset when you want to speedrun the opening minutes with better intuition.

Strategy: why “Minecraft brain” helps

Real Minecraft teaches tool tier gating: you don’t rush obsidian with bare hands. This clicker copies that feel numerically—each tier is a bigger passive multiplier, so you experience the same “finally iron!” dopamine, abstracted into exponential block counters.

  • Opportunity cost: every second without the best affordable passive online is blocks you never get back—idle games punish perfectionist hoarding.
  • Parallel vs vertical: sometimes five extra Stone axes beat one delayed Diamond because cost curves reward breadth early.
  • Session types: short sessions favor click power; long AFK sessions favor stacked beacon + table throughput.

Who this is for

Speedrunners who want a low-friction incremental between Realms sessions, students on locked-down Chromebooks, or anyone who likes watching resource numbers climb with Minecraft-flavored nouns. It’s also a gentle entry point to the genre if you’ve never opened an idle spreadsheet in your life—just don’t expect redstone sim depth; expect satisfying arithmetic.
